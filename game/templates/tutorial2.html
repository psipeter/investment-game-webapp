Let's talk strategy!

In the Investment Game, there is a tradeoff between immediate reward and long-term reputation

To demonstrate this, we'll play two mini-games (3 turns)

In the first game, you'll be the investor and we'll force you to make generous investments

In the second game, you'll be the trustee and we'll for you to make greedy returns

You've just finished your first game!

By investing generously, both you and the trustee have collected a good number of coins

Your final score is X and the instructor's score is Y

If, instead, you had chosen to keep everything and invest nothing, the final scores would have been 30 and 0

This shows that investing generously can benefit both players ... but what if the trustee gets greedy?

In this game, you'll play as the trustee

We'll force you to make greedy decisions, and see what happens ...

When the trustee is greedy, they can collect many coins on a single turn

However, this action may anger the investor, causing them to give less next turn

In this game, the instructor punished your greedy actions, preventing you from collecting many coins later in the game

You can check the Game Log at the bottom to see this for yourself
